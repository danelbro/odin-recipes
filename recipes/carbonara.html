Carbonara

Description

This classic Roman pasta dish is notoriously difficult to master.

It's a deliciously creamy sauce, with crisp fatty pancetta and lots of freshly ground black pepper, but it uses no cream; all the creaminess comes from the eggs.

Here's a method I've honed for a few years, which seems pretty authentic.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti or linguine
  • Large eggs - for 2 servings, 3 yolks + 1 whole egg
  • Pancetta
  • Parmesan
  • Pecorino
  • Pepper
  • Butter

Steps

  1. Heat a pan over medium heat and cook the pancetta in a little olive oil. When crispy and golden, leave aside to cool, reserving some of the fat.
  2. Cook the pasta in well-salted water according to manufacturer's instructions.
  3. While the pasta is cooking, put the egg yolks and egg into a large mixing bowl, preferably metal. Add a good amount of finely grated parmesan and pecorino and freshly ground black pepper to the mix, and combine.
  4. If the bowl is metal, heat the egg mixture gently by placing the bowl on top of the pasta pan. Don't cook the eggs: you want them to be slightly warm so adding the cooked pasta to them doesnt shock them into scrambling.
  5. Add the reserved pancetta and a little of the fat to the egg mixture.
  6. When the pasta is cooked, reserve some of the starchy water and drain. Add chopped butter to the egg mixture and a splash of pasta water. Then, add pasta to the mixture and mix vigorously with tongs (for longer than you think). Add a little more pasta water if necessary until you have a creamy sauce which coats each strand of pasta.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste, and serve with grated parmesan and more pepper.
